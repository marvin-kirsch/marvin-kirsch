Areas I'm interested in:

At the moment, I am especially interested in how Rust can be used for high-performance simulations, embedded development, and technical tooling.

### Quantum Computing

I'm exploring quantum computing from the ground up.

Current focus:

- understanding qubits
- complex numbers and amplitudes
- normalization
- quantum gates
- basic simulation of quantum systems
- building intuition before relying on frameworks

The goal is not only to use quantum computing libraries, but to understand enough of the fundamentals to build small educational simulations myself.

### Embedded Development

I'm experimenting with embedded systems, especially with microcontrollers and low-level hardware interaction.

Current interests:

- embedded Rust
- Teensy / ARM-based boards

- eventually: TinyML and computer vision on constrained devices

### Drones

When not debugging software, I am probably debugging something that flies.

Current drone-related interests:

- building and flying drones
- understanding flight controllers
- experimenting with hardware setups

Drones are a nice mix of electronics, software, physics, and chaos.

## My setup

### NixOS

I use NixOS as my main system.

Because reproducible systems are good, declarative configuration is powerful, and sometimes rebuilding your entire system from a flake is exactly the kind of pain that feels worth it.

Current Linux interests:

* NixOS flakes
* reproducible development environments
* Hyprland / Wayland
* Neovim-based workflows
* clean terminal setups
* declarative tooling
* keeping my system useful without turning it into a second full-time job

NixOS is the best. :)
